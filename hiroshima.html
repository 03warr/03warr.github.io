Hiroshima

Onomichi Ramen

Onomichi is an idyllic town in Hiroshima
Prefecture that's known for its cycling routes and cats,
including two internet-famous cats that keep
trying to enter one of the local museums.

Its local ramen style uses a chicken-soy sauce
broth base that's infused with seafood extracts,
as Onomichi faces the Seto Inland Sea.

The noodles are flat and semi-thick, and they're
usually served with the usual ramen toppings -
menma, chashu, and so on - plus generous heaps
of se-abura (pork fat). Like at Wakayama, the
ramen here is also typically called "chuka soba."

Ingredients

1 piece kombu (kelp)

1/2 cup katsuobushi (bonito flakes)

5 ounces niboshi (dried anchovies sardines)

5 ounces dried shiitake mushrooms

1 tablespoon minced pork back fat

1 tablespoon sesame oil

1 clove garlic, finely grated

2 teaspoons grated ginger

3 tablespoons shoyu (Japanese soy sauce)

1 teaspoon mirin

10 ounces fresh flat ramen noodles

4 pieces of menma (fermented bamboo shoots)

2 slices chashu
(pork shoulder or belly simmered in soy sauce and mirin)

1 ramen egg, sliced lengthwise

2 scallions, thinly sliced
