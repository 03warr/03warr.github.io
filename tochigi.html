Sano Ramen

Sano ramen is characterized by chewy,
wrinkly-looking, semi-thick noodles.

These are made with a unique method
called aodake-uchi: using a long, thick
bamboo roller to press the dough, giving
the noodles their distinctive texture. The
noodles are then served with a clear
soy-sauce-and-chicken broth.

Sano is a quiet commuter town, but like
with Kitakata, people occasionally make
pilgrimages here just for the ramen!

Ingredients

1/4 ounce kombu, or dried kelp
(7g; about one 7- by 2-inch piece)

1/4 ounce dried shiitake mushrooms
(7g; about 2 to 3 mushrooms)

1 ounce (30g) shaved katsuobushi
(dried bonito) flakes

2 quarts (1.8L) chintan chicken stock

1/2 cup (120ml) shio tare

4 servings ramen noodles,
either homemade or store-bought

1/2 cup (120ml) aromatic oil, schmaltz,
or lard, heated until liquid

Toppings, such as chashu or
ajitsuke tamago (optional)

2 scallions, sliced as thinly as possible
