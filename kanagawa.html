Yokohama

Ramen from the port city of Yokohama
is also known as ie-kei (home-style)
ramen. A fairly recent style that emerged
in 1974, Yokohama ramen is not (yet) as
well-known as other regional ramen vari-
eties. It has a cult following, though, and
may well be on its way to reaching
mainstream popularity.

This ramen uses a hybrid shoyu-
tonkotsu base, which, while not
uncommon now, was considered
innovative in the 1970s.

Ingredients

3 lbs pork bones

2 lbs pork trotters (split in half)

water

1 leek (optional)

1 lb pork belly

1/3 cup soy sauce

1 and1/4 cup sake

1 tbsp mirin

2 tbsp brown sugar
(or palm sugar or honey)

1/2 cup spinach (fried or boiled)

1 green onion (sliced)

2 eggs (softboiled)

2 packages ramen noodles

1 and 1/2 tbsp chicken fat

1 green onion (sliced)

3 garlic cloves (minced)
