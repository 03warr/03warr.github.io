Fukuoka

Hakata Ramen

Named after the part of Fukuoka that was a
major trading port in ancient Japan, Hakata
ramen is practically synonymous with tonkotsu.
Not all tonkotsu ramen is Hakata ramen,
though; the Hakata style is, specifically, a very
thick and creamy kind of tonkotsu ramen.

The complete opposite of Kitakata ramen,
Hakata ramen noodles are thin, as they're made
with more flour than water. Common toppings
include chashu pork, scallions, pickled ginger,
and kikurage (cloud-ear mushrooms).

Ingredients

3 lbs pork bones

1 leek (sliced)

1 onion (quartered)

1 garlic head (cut in half)

3 in ginger (sliced)

water

2 tbsp sesame oil

1 tbsp mirin

1/2 cup clam juice

1.6 lbs pork belly (skin on, no bone)

2 and 1/2 tbsp brown sugar

1 tbsp gochujang

1 and 1/2 tbsp soy sauce

1 tbsp mirin

1 dash Chinese 5 spice powder

2 green onion (sliced)

3 garlic cloves (sliced)

3 tsp black sesame seeds

3 tsp pickled ginger

3 somen noodles (packages)
